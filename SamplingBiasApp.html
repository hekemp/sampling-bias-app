Random Sampling with Bias


In this app, you will draw a random sample from the population of all GalootsImagine Big Alien Chickens. on Planet ZA far away planet ideal for practicing statistical methods..

Step 1: Choose the variable you want to graph:  WeightTo keep things simple, all weights are reported in pounds based on standard Earth gravity. Cares for OffspringThis variable has a value of 1 for any Galoot who cares for their offspring, otherwise the value is 0.

Step 2: Choose the sample size you want to use:  10 40 160

A Galoot

Step 3: Explore the effects of biased samplingSampling bias results from flaws in the sampling process resulting in some segment of individuals in the population of interest having less chance (or no chance) of being selected for inclusion in the sample. by specifying the wayTo see the impact of one of the pre-determined types of sampling bias, use the dropdown window to select a specific type of bias. in which the bias will be present in the data.

Note: Changing the settings in step 2 or step 3 will clear all previous results.

Step 4: Click on "Draw a sample" to draw your first sample of Galoots.

Step 5: Click “Store result” to write the sample mean to the data table in the lower left. This also adds the sample mean to the empirical sampling distribution on the lower right, and draws a new random sample of the same size.

Note: Step 4 can be repeated as many times as you like. The more data you add to the empirical sampling distribution, the more it will accurately represent the theoretical sampling distribution for the sample mean.

Anytime you want to start over you can either click on "Clear all data" or change either the variable or sample size.
